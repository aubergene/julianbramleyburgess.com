Julian Bramley Burgess explores equilibria and geometry in sculptural and digital forms. His work includes hanging mobile installations, mechanically-plotted prints, and paintings.

His mobiles emerge from whippletree structures formed from regular polygons. Finding balance is a key part to this work, which looks at hierarchies and complex relationships. He uses his background in software engineering to understand the principles and limits behind these relations.

His prints and drawings build on ideas of dreams, entropy, and the collective unconscious. In the work Dreamspace, he harvested over a hundred thousand dream diary entries. These were then fed into a high-dimensional space using a machine learning algorithm. The resulting semantic space was then projected and plotted in the familiar form of a stereographic celestial map, where the stars are the objects of dreams and the constellations the links between us all.

Burgess graduated with an MA in Computational Art from Goldsmiths College in 2020. His work has been exhibited at the Horniman Museum, London and HoloCenter Center for the Holographic Arts in New York. His work is based at Proposition Studios in Camden.
